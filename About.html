    GUITAR PICKUP

    A guitar pickup device is a transducer (specifically, a variable
    reluctance sensor) that captures or senses mechanical vibrations produced by
    stringed instruments such such as the guitar and converts them
    to an electrical signal that is amplified using an instrument
    amplifier to produce musical sounds through a loudspeaker in a
    speaker enclosure. Most newbie enthusiasts are unaware that semi-acoustic
    does not mean that a guitar is anything less
    than an acoustic one. In fact, it is simply an
    acoustic guitar fixed with a pickup device. Every Signature Musicals guitar
    comes with a passive pickup which you can connect to
    either an amplifier or a speaker. We also supply
    active pickups on request. This functionality is absent in copycat
    and fake products sold by dubious sellers.

    QUALITY

    WOOD AND STRING QUALITY

    Dalbergia, also known by the names Sheesham wood and Indian Rosewood
    is the penultimate quality of wood bested only by the pure
    Brazilian Rosewood which is a tad warmer than the former.
    They sound slightly different, but both just as good
    and although they are found in totally different geographies,
    there has been no equal for the sweet sound of
    the Dalbergia in south-east Asia and India.
    The genus Toona Ciliata, commonly known as the red cedar,
    is native to India and is used for high-grade
    export-quality musical instruments. It is considered to be of
    a grade higher than its siblings such as the
    Toona Sinensis found in China, Toona Sureni found in Indonesia
    and Toona Calantis found in the archipelago of Philippines.
    It is considered at par with the Cuban Cedrela Odorata
    and the Mexican Guarea Grandifolia, which are considered to be paramount
    in quality for the manufacturing of musical instruments.
    The wood used in our guitar making is derived from
    the direct strain that was initially planted as a plantation crop
    in Hawaii in 1918 using seeds from Australia and has
    since become naturalized. Such quality is peerless in the entire
    Indian sub-continent and is sought-after by guitar enthusiasts
    not only in India but worldwide.
    Classical guitar strings are typically nylon, with the basses being
    wound with either silver or bronze (with phosphor) . Electric guitar strings
    are usually wound with nickel plated steel; pure nickel
    and stainless steel are also used. Bass guitar strings are
    most commonly wound with stainless steel or nickel.
    On the other hand, strings on the Signature Musicals guitars
    are made up of a metal that is treated electrochemically
    such that it possesses the strength of cryogenically frozen strings,
    which seems to lengthen their lifespan without diminishing the tone
    or sustain metal. The string gauge is optimal for guitar playing
    so that they retain their strength even when played for
    longer periods of time. This is especially useful for beginners
    as they are more likely to break the strings
    while practicing and playing.
